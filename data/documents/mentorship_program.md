# Thrive Scholars Mentorship Program

## Overview

The Thrive Scholars Mentorship Program connects current scholars with experienced professionals and Thrive alumni to provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities throughout the college journey and beyond.

## Types of Mentorship

### Academic Mentors
Academic mentors are current college students or recent graduates who provide:
- Study strategies and time management tips
- Course selection guidance
- Major exploration support
- Research opportunity connections
- Graduate school preparation advice

### Career Mentors
Career mentors are professionals with 5+ years of experience who offer:
- Industry insights and career path guidance
- Resume and LinkedIn profile reviews
- Mock interviews and feedback
- Networking introductions
- Internship and job referrals

### Life Mentors
Life mentors focus on personal development and include:
- Thrive Scholars alumni
- Community leaders
- Life coaches
- Mental health advocates

## How the Program Works

### Matching Process
1. Complete the mentorship interest form
2. Indicate your goals and preferences
3. Our team reviews and matches you with compatible mentors
4. Attend a virtual introduction session
5. Begin regular meetings with your mentor

### Meeting Expectations
- **Frequency**: At least once per month
- **Duration**: 30-60 minutes per session
- **Format**: Video call, phone, or in-person (when possible)
- **Communication**: Respond to mentor messages within 48 hours

### Mentee Responsibilities
- Come prepared with questions and topics
- Complete agreed-upon action items
- Be punctual and respectful of mentor's time
- Provide feedback on the mentorship experience
- Update your mentor on progress and achievements

## Mentorship Timeline

### First Year (Freshman)
- Focus: College transition and academic success
- Mentor Type: Academic mentor (peer or near-peer)
- Goals: Build study habits, explore interests, get involved on campus

### Second Year (Sophomore)
- Focus: Major selection and career exploration
- Mentor Type: Academic + beginning career mentorship
- Goals: Declare major, explore internships, build skills

### Third Year (Junior)
- Focus: Career preparation and professional development
- Mentor Type: Career mentor (industry professional)
- Goals: Secure internship, build professional network, develop expertise

### Fourth Year (Senior)
- Focus: Job search and post-graduation planning
- Mentor Type: Career mentor + alumni mentor
- Goals: Full-time job or graduate school acceptance

## Becoming a Mentor

Thrive alumni and professionals can apply to become mentors:

### Requirements
- Bachelor's degree (or current college senior)
- Minimum 2 years post-graduation for career mentors
- Background check completion
- Mentor training workshop attendance
- Commitment to at least one academic year

### Benefits of Mentoring
- Give back to the Thrive community
- Develop leadership and coaching skills
- Expand your professional network
- Access to exclusive Thrive events
- Recognition at annual Thrive Gala

## FAQs

### Q: Can I have multiple mentors?
A: Yes! Many scholars have both an academic and career mentor simultaneously.

### Q: What if my mentor isn't a good fit?
A: Contact the mentorship team, and we'll work with you to find a better match.

### Q: How long does mentorship last?
A: Mentor relationships can last throughout your college career and beyond. Many scholar-mentor pairs maintain lifelong connections.

### Q: Is mentorship required?
A: While not required, we strongly encourage all scholars to participate. Studies show mentored students have higher graduation rates and career success.
